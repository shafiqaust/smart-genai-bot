PAGE 2 – Self-Evolving Agents (Definition)
================================================================================
Topic: Self-Evolving AI Agents – Definition

Self-evolving AI agents are autonomous systems that continuously and systematically optimize their internal components through interaction with environments, with the goal of adapting to changing tasks, contexts and resources while preserving safety and enhancing performance.

================================================================================
PAGE 3 – Three Laws of Self-Evolving AI Agents
================================================================================
Topic: Three Laws of Self-Evolving AI Agents

LAW 1 – Endure (Safety Adaptation):
Self-evolving AI agents must maintain safety and stability during any modification.

LAW 2 – Excel (Performance Preservation):
Subject to the First Law, self-evolving AI agents must preserve or enhance existing task performance.

LAW 3 – Evolve (Autonomous Evolution):
Subject to the First and Second Law, self-evolving AI agents must be able to autonomously optimize their internal components in response to changing tasks, environments, or resources.

Note: The three laws follow a priority order — safety first, performance second, autonomous evolution third.

PAGE 4 – Classification of AI Agent Paradigms
================================================================================
Topic: Classification of AI Agent Paradigms

Four main paradigms:

1. Model Offline Pretraining (MOP)
   - Interaction & Feedback: Model ↔ Static data (loss/backprop)
   - Key Techniques:
     * Transformer Pretraining (Causal LM, Masked LM, NSP)
     * BPE / SentencePiece
     * MoE & Pipeline Parallelism

2. Model Online Adaptation (MOA)
   - Interaction & Feedback: Model ↔ Supervision (labels/scores/rewards)
   - Key Techniques:
     * Task Fine-tuning
     * Instruction Tuning
     * LoRA / Adapters / Prefix-Tuning
     * RLHF (RLAIF, DPO, PPO)
     * Multi-Modal Alignment
     * Human Alignment

3. Multi-Agent Orchestration (MAO)
   - Interaction & Feedback: Agent₁ ↔ Agent₂ (message exchange)
   - Key Techniques:
     * Multi-Agent Systems
     * Self-Reflection
     * Multi-Agent Debate
     * Chain-of-Thought Ensemble
     * Function / Tool Calling / MCP

4. Multi-Agent Self-Evolving (MASE)
   - Interaction & Feedback: Agents ↔ Environment (signals from env.)
   - Key Techniques:
     * Behaviour Optimisation
     * Prompt Optimisation
     * Memory Optimisation
     * Tool Optimisation
     * Agentic Workflow Optimisation

PAGE 8 – Multi-Agent Architecture Types
================================================================================
Topic: Multi-Agent System Architectures

Three architecture types:

1. Centralized (Centralize):
   - All agents connect directly to a single Control Center
   - Hub-and-spoke topology

2. Hierarchical:
   - Control Center → Region Agents → Zone Agents → Switch Agents → Sub-agents
   - Tree-structured topology with multiple levels

3. Decentralized (Distributed):
   - Control Center exists but agents have cross-connections
   - Switch Agents interconnect with multiple peers
   - Mesh-like topology

PAGE 10 – Limitations of Current Systems and Introduction to MASE
================================================================================
Topic: Limitations of MOP/MOA/MAO and Motivation for MASE

Current limitation:
The trajectory from Model Offline Pretraining (MOP) through Model Online Adaptation (MOA) and Multi-Agent Orchestration (MAO) has steadily reduced manual configuration in LLM-based systems. Yet even the most advanced multi-agent frameworks today often depend on:
- Handcrafted workflows
- Fixed communication protocols
- Human-curated toolchains

Solution – Multi-Agent Self-Evolving (MASE) systems:
- Agents autonomously refine their prompts, memory, tool-use strategies, and interaction topology
- Guided by feedback from the environment and higher-level meta-rewards
- Continuous optimization enables agents not merely to adapt once, but to evolve over their lifetime
- Adapts to shifting tasks, domains, and operational constraints

Key concept: Lifelong, self-evolving agentic systems embed a continuous improvement loop into the core of the architecture.

PAGE 12 – Single-Agent Optimization
================================================================================
Topic: Single-Agent Optimization Categories

Four optimization areas:

1. LLM Behavior Optimization:
   - Goal: Improve LLM's reasoning and planning capabilities
   - Methods: Parameter tuning OR test-time scaling techniques
   - Sub-types:
     * Reasoning Behaviour Optimisation
     * Test-Time Scaling Optimisation
       - Feedback-based Strategy
       - Search-based Strategy

2. Prompt Optimization:
   - Goal: Adapt prompts to guide LLM toward more accurate and task-relevant outputs
   - Sub-types:
     * Edit-Based Optimisation
     * Generative Optimisation
     * Text Gradient-Based Optimisation
     * Evolutionary Optimisation

3. Memory Optimization:
   - Goal: Enhance agent's ability to store, retrieve, and reason over historical information or external knowledge
   - Sub-types:
     * Short-term Memory Optimisation
     * Long-term Memory Optimisation

4. Tool Optimization:
   - Goal: Enhance agent's ability to effectively leverage existing tools, or autonomously create or configure new tools to accomplish complex tasks
   - Sub-types:
     * Training-Based Optimisation
     * Inference-Time Optimisation
     * Prompt-Based Tool Optimisation
     * Reasoning-Based Tool Optimisation

================================================================================
Topic: Domain-Specific Optimization

Concept: Specific domains have unique requirements that demand tailored optimization strategies.

Domains covered:

1. Biomedicine:
   - Challenge: Aligning agents' behavior with the procedural and operational requirements of real-world clinical settings.

2. Programming:
   - Challenge: Following established software engineering workflow (code refinement & debugging).

3. Financial Reasoning:
   - Challenge: Handling uncertain and rapidly changing conditions, reasoning over volatile market dynamics, and integrating heterogeneous information sources such as numerical indicators, news sentiment, and expert knowledge.

   - Challenge: Interpreting structured legal rules, analyzing case-specific evidence, and producing outputs that are consistent with institutional regulations and judicial standards.
