SECTION: Module Title
Module: Diffusion Models

---

SECTION: Overview
In this module, we will learn about Diffusion Models, the type of models behind all the famous image generation systems such as DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, etc. We will discuss the motivation behind this type of models, its architecture, and an implementation of a diffusion model that generates images of flowers.

---

SECTION: Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, you should be able to:

MLO 1: Learn the underlying principles and components that define a diffusion model.

MLO 2: Understand the reparameterization trick and why it is important.

MLO 3: Explore different forms of forward diffusion scheduling. Understand the reverse diffusion process and how it relates to the forward noising process.

MLO 4: Explore the architecture of the U-net, which is used to parameterize the reverse diffusion process.

MLO 5: Build your own denoising diffusion model (DDM) images of flowers using Keras to generate flowers, sample new images of flowers from your model.

MLO 6: Explore the effect of the number of diffusion steps on image quality and interpolate between two images in the latent space.
